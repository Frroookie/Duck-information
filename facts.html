Duck fun facts

Stuff I've picked up at the pond and from way too much casual reading. Good for bringing up at parties, mostly to mixed reactions.

Things worth knowing

  • A group of ducks on water is called a "raft." A group on land or in flight is usually just called a "flock."
  • Ducklings can swim within hours of hatching — they don't need to be taught.
  • A duck's feathers are coated in oil from a gland near the tail, which is why water beads off rather than soaking in.
  • Some ducks, like the wood duck and bufflehead, nest in tree cavities rather than on the ground.
  • Ducks have a wide field of vision thanks to eyes set on the sides of their head — great for spotting predators, less great for binocular depth perception straight ahead.
  • Migratory ducks can travel thousands of miles between breeding and wintering grounds each year.

Pond etiquette: bread isn't actually good for ducks — it fills them up without much nutritional value. Cracked corn, oats, or peas are much better if you want to feed the ones at your local pond.
